Gardening Project

Our gardening project is designed to combat isolation among elderly and isolated women, offering them a welcoming space to step outside, engage in meaningful outdoor activities, and form connections with others. These interactions not only provide participants with the opportunity to make new friends but also give them a much-needed break from the isolation that many elderly women face. By working together in the garden, they experience the therapeutic benefits of nature, which improves both their physical and mental well-being. Additionally, the project goes beyond personal support by encouraging environmental awareness within the wider community. Participants are introduced to sustainable gardening practices, such as growing their own food, which promotes healthy eating while reducing reliance on store-bought produce. This hands-on experience fosters a deeper understanding of the environment and encourages people to consider their ecological footprint. As the project flourishes, it inspires a collective effort to create a greener, more sustainable community. By tackling isolation and raising environmental consciousness, we are nurturing a more connected, healthy, and environmentally responsible community in Islington.
